Their dating story. In October of 2015 Julie's cousin, Sam Fredin, asked Julie's permission to introduce her to someone. Julie, not thinking much of the proposition said, "sure". She had almost forgotten about it when she was introduced to Erik Detlefsen. They exchanged messages for a week. When he asked her if they could meet, she looked at her schedule and thought to herself, "if we actually meet, it will be a miricle." As her schedule stood then, she had three obscure times to meet. Erik made it happen. They set their first date for a lovely Sunday evening in autumn. At the Gutherie Theater they ran into mutual friends. The evening ran late as the group chatted, removing all awkwardness that can surround a first date. The continued to keep in touch after their date. A week later, Julie, the jet-setter and adventuerer was sitting in a hotel, having gone away for the weekend. A relization came upon her that she had no desire to be anywhere but in Minneapolis spending time with Erik. Though they both keep inhumanly busy schedules, making time for one another seemed natural. Their hobbies and lifestyles fit perfectly together: one evening they would be dressing up and enjoying the city, the next evening they would be down in Florida golfing, another evening they would be driving up to watch the northern lights. Both families welcomed the buding relationship, accepting the other into their family. On a weekend in July, Erik's family was going up north to Canada. Julie, having just started a new job didn't think Erik would propose to her until things had settled down. All weekend, Erik carried around the ring looking for the right moment. It never came. As the two drove home together they stopped at the same spot they had their first opened up to one another with deep conversation under the northern lights. Julie sat on the back of the truck, although there were no northern lights that evening, Erik couldn't wait another moment. He knelt down in front of her and asked Julie if she would spend the rest of her life with him. She said, "yes."
